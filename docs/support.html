Sonority — Support

Sonority configures compatible speakers on your local Wi-Fi in ways the standard controller app doesn't expose. Need help? Email casperverswijveltdev@gmail.com and include your device, OS version, and speaker models.

No speakers found

Your phone or Mac and your speakers must be on the same Wi-Fi network (not a guest network, and not split across 2.4/5 GHz SSIDs that block local traffic). On first launch, allow the Local Network permission prompt — without it the OS silently blocks discovery.

Which speaker is which?

Tap the lightbulb button to blink a speaker's status light. On iPhone/iPad you can also tap the speaker button to play a short chime. (The chime isn't available on Mac.)

Did I break my setup?

No — every change Sonority makes is a standard, supported speaker bonding, is shown to you before it's applied, and can be undone from within the app. You can also rebuild any configuration from the manufacturer's own app.

Room-calibration tuning

Sonority can switch an existing tuning on or off, but it does not measure a new one — measurement is done in the manufacturer's own app. Changing a bonded set can cause the manufacturer's system to clear an existing tuning; that's the manufacturer's behavior, not something Sonority can restore.
